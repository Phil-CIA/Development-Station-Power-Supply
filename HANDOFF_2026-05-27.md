## What Was Resolved This Session

### Root Cause: NMOS FETs in Wrong Orientation (Q1–Q12)

- Schematic was designed for PMOS (source-high, gate-low = ON).
- Physically installed parts are N-channel (LCSC C22469448, HL3400 clone, Vth ≈ 900mV).
- NMOS source pins connect to +5V_reg → turning on requires Vgate > 5.9V.
- 74HC595 max output = 3.3V → **FETs have never conducted via their channels**.
- The 4.74V drain node reading was body diode equilibrium, not channel conduction.
- **Fix**: Board redesign (planned). No firmware workaround is possible.

### J4 Jumper (non-schematic, must stay)

- Jumper from J4 pin 4 ("Feedback 5V") to J4 pin 7 ("+5V_reg") is the **only** power delivery path to HAT board outputs.
- Removing it without working FETs causes regulator overvoltage (FB pin floats low → duty cycle ramps to max).
- **Leave the jumper installed until the board redesign is complete.**
